Abstract

In response to the complex task of selecting optimal beach and resort destinations in the Zamboanga Peninsula, this study introduced a groundbreaking web-based application. Our research aimed to revolutionize decision-making methodologies by integrating Multi-Criteria Decision Making (MCDM) to assess key factors such as cleanliness, safety, accessibility, natural beauty, recreational activities, and accommodation. With a focus on user-centric design, we actively engaged travel enthusiasts for continuous feedback and customization, ensuring the application aligned with their preferences. Data collection employed Google Forms for distributed questionnaires, while statistical tools encompassed MCDM, descriptive, and inferential statistics. Employing an Agile software process model, technical tools like GPT API, APIFY, Visual Studio Code, Google Forms, and Microsoft Excel contributed to the application's development. The study's significance extended beyond user satisfaction, aiming to stimulate growth in the local tourism industry and provide valuable insights for academics, businesses, and policymakers. The conceptual framework integrated Facebook web scraping and sentiment analysis, resulting in a robust decision-making system. Findings from user feedback and data analysis were presented in the Results and Discussion section, highlighting the transformative impact on travel decisions in Zamboanga Peninsula. This study was not merely a technological advancement but a catalyst for informed and enjoyable travel experiences in the region.
